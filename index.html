Page 1: Science Explanation

What is Forensic Biotechnology?

Forensic biotechnology uses DNA and biological evidence to identify people in criminal investigations. It analyzes genetic material from crime scenes.

Types of DNA

Nuclear DNA: Found in the cell nucleus, used for identification.

Mitochondrial DNA: Passed from mother, used when DNA is old or damaged.

Biological Evidence

  • Blood
  • Saliva
  • Hair
  • Skin cells (touch DNA)
  • Bones and teeth

Genetic Markers

STRs identify individuals. SNPs help with ancestry and degraded samples.

Page 2: Investigations

Uses of DNA Technology

  • Identify suspects
  • Identify victims
  • Exonerate innocent people
  • Missing persons cases

Accuracy

DNA matching is extremely accurate, often billions-to-one probability.

CODIS Database

CODIS is a national DNA database used by law enforcement to match crime scene DNA.

Chain of Custody

Evidence must be tracked from collection to court to prevent contamination or tampering.

Page 3: Gabby Petito Case

Case Overview

Gabby Petito disappeared in 2021 while traveling across the U.S. Her remains were later found in Wyoming.

DNA Analysis

  • DNA extracted from remains
  • PCR used to amplify DNA
  • STR profiling used for identification
  • Matched with family DNA

Outcome

DNA confirmed her identity and helped investigators close the case.

Impact

Raised awareness of domestic violence and improved missing persons response systems.
